Rebellion

General

Since DMC3 the Rebellion's moveset has stayed very consistent, meaning it's very easy to become familiar with from how much it appears. Rebellion is a good all-rounded weapon, with attacks that come out fairly quickly dealing decent damage with decently fast multi-hit combos. DMC3's Devil Trigger gives you specific boosts based on the current weapon Devil Arm you're using, for Rebellion DT gives a decent boost to all stats making it good and well rounded.

Dance Macabre Lock + Down + B (mash) Does a faster version of Combo I and Combo II before ending with a Million Stab.
Crazy Dance Crazy Dance Macabre Dante plants his sword in the ground and spins around on it repeatedly, dealing damage to the enemies he touches, before ending in a very strong swing.

Rebellion has more moves than most of Dante's melee weapons in DMC3, with some moves being crucial for combos, like Aerial Rave, which is one of the most commonly used moves in combos throughout the series, being easy to jump cancel to stall the enemy in the air once the timing is right. The move High Time is one of the most consistent launcher attacks, having the option to follow the enemy airborne. It's A and B combos are good multi-hit moves which are still fast enough to be usable in a combo. Stinger/Million Stab are also very good for closing in on enemies if you can pull off the Crazy Combo for Stinger. Drive and Dance Macabre are both very good moves for finishers. All round, Rebellion is a very consistent and reliable weapon that is very useful for combos.
