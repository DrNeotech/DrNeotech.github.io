Styles

General

The addition of the style system in DMC3 opened up the opportunities to expand Dante's moveset and versatility in a way that would become a mainstay of the series after this game. The style system allows Dante to be more mobile and evade, block enemy attacks with rewards for good timing, or expand his melee or gun weapon moveset with more stylish attacks. It's important to note that even though DMC3 did introduce styles and instant weapon switching, the ability to switch styles on the go and switch between more than two weapons was only introduced in DMC4, and only the Nintendo Switch release of DMC3 has these built in, as well as the PC releases of DMC3 which have mods that enable these, for both DMC3:SE on PC and the HD Collection. If you install these mods I recommend making a backup save since in my case it messed with my save file for my first playthrough. For these pages I'll be assuming you're using a version which allows for style switching since that's the only one I have a proper familiarity with (I've only played DMC3 once without weapon and style switching, the rest on the Switch).

Trickster

Trickster style is primarily built for evading attacks and closing in on enemies, making it a crucial feature for stylish combat in DMC. Trickster gives Dante a ground dash with a lot of invincibility frames, which can be used three times consecutively upon reaching level 3, an aerial dash which can be used once, and an air trick which teleports to the enemy. In every DMC game Trickster plays a crucial role in keeping combos flowing, as these abilities allow Dante to close in on enemies and position himself relative to them to set up attacks. There isn't much to say about it since it isn't very technically tricky once you can get the hang of quickly switching styles to use it on the fly (if you're using a style switcher mod or the Switch version). Also remember that although Trickster is the most convenient way of closing in on enemies there are probably more stylish options available, but for the most part it's recommended.

Swordmaster

Swordmaster is another crucial style for stylish combos, expanding the number of abilities for Dante's Devil Arms and providing lots of combo potential. Attacks that are fundamental to Dante's combat, like Aerial Rave on Rebellion can only be used on Swordmaster. Overall it gives all of Dante's melee weapons a new set of stylish moves for every weapon. If no mods are used for style switching Swordmaster will probably be your go to style, since without it you're locked out of around 40% of Dante's Devil Arm attacks (27 out of 71 attacks).

Gunslinger

One of the mistakes often made in beginner combos is not using Dante's guns enough, since they are a very useful tool for extending combos. This is why Gunslinger is an important style to become familiar as it Gunslinger expands Dante's firearm attacks. While firearms aren't as useful as melee weapons in DMC, the firearms are useful for positioning enemies and stalling their movement, with Ebony and Ivory being able to stagger enemies mid-air while you prepare for an attack, or the shotgun to create distance between you and the enemy. Gunslinger makes these firearms more powerful and versatile and it should not be overlooked.

Royal Guard

In normal playthroughs Royal Guard is one of the most rewarding techniques in the game to master using, allowing you to block enemy attacks and punish with devastating damage. However for stylish combos it's much less useful, as enemies are usually being attacked and stunned too long for them to land an attack. At most you could block an attack before a combo to stun the enemy and create an opening for a combo or possibly use a Just Release to launch the enemy into the air. That's all the uses that I'm aware of as of now, but if you know any others contact me to tell me and I'll probably add it.

Quicksilver

Quicksilver is a style unlocked later in the game which lets Dante slow down time, at the cost of draining lots of DT. This can be much more useful combined with a costume that gives infinite DT, but otherwise you'll find yourself running out of meter very quickly. Quicksilver can allow Dante to perform attacks that would usually require more set up, like Real Impact, without leaving the enemy to touch the ground and make the combo less cool.

Doppelganger

Purely damage as far as I know, no clue how you could use this for combos outside of the secret multiplayer where another person can control the doppelganger.
